Cloudiness

This graph shows the relative Cloudiness of each sample cities compared against those cities' relative Latitudes. From the graph, we can clearly see that there is no definitive correlation between the two factors.

Cloudiness is generally affected by factors largely directly unrelated to distance from the equator: local geography or terrain, humidity, air temperature are just a few relevant factors. We also look at the relationship between distance to the equator and humidity for our sample of cities. Perhaps there is some stronger correlationship between the three, as humidity affects cloud formation.
